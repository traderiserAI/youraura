AI Transparency: How Our Models Work

Last Updated: October 27, 2025

Our Commitment: Most wellness apps treat their AI as a "black box." We think that's wrong. You deserve to know exactly how our AI works, what data it uses, and its limitations.

The 5 AI Models

1. K-Means Clustering (Archetype Classifier)

What it does: Matches you with one of 5 wellness archetypes based on your quiz responses.

How it works: Compares your answers to thousands of other users' profiles and finds which cluster (archetype) you're closest to.

Data used: Your 7 quiz responses only

Accuracy: ~85% of users report their archetype "feels accurate"

Limitations: Not a diagnosis. Simplified model of complex human beings. Based on current state, not permanent identity.

2. Q-Learning (Adaptive Quiz)

What it does: Chooses which question to ask next based on your previous answers.

How it works: Reinforcement learning algorithm that learns the "best question" to ask in each situation.

Data used: Your answers to previous questions + patterns from thousands of quiz completions

Accuracy: Reaches 85% classification accuracy in 7 questions (vs. 10-12 with static quiz)

Limitations: Requires at least 3 answers to start adapting. May occasionally ask redundant questions.

3. LSTM (Behavioral Pattern Analyzer)

What it does: Analyzes 14 days of your check-ins to surface early stress signals.

How it works: Neural network that finds patterns in sequences of data over time (like rising stress + declining energy).

Data used: 14 days of your self-reported mood, energy, and stress levels

Accuracy: 70% of users who received alerts agreed they were feeling more stressed

Limitations:

  • ‚ùå NOT predictive medicine - this is pattern recognition, not medical prediction
  • ‚ùå Requires 14 days of data to provide insights
  • ‚ùå Cannot diagnose burnout, depression, or any medical condition
  • ‚ùå 20% false positive rate (alerts when you feel fine)

Important: This model surfaces informational insights based on patterns in your self-reported data. It is NOT a diagnostic tool and cannot predict future health outcomes with certainty.

4. RecBole (Recommendation Engine)

What it does: Suggests personalized daily recommendations (yoga, meditation, CBT exercises).

How it works: Collaborative filtering (like Netflix) - learns from your engagement and similar users' preferences.

Data used: Your archetype + your engagement with past recommendations + aggregated data from similar users

Accuracy: 40% of recommended practices are completed (vs. 15% for random recommendations)

Limitations: Less accurate in first week (not enough data about your preferences). May create "filter bubble" by over-recommending things you already like.

5. Conversational AI (Chatbot)

What it does: Provides 24/7 informational support via chat in English or Telugu.

How it works: Large language model (Llama 3 or BlenderBot) fine-tuned on wellness conversations.

Data used: Your messages + your archetype + recent check-ins (for context) + conversation history

Accuracy: 85% of users rate responses as "helpful" or "very helpful"

Limitations:

  • ‚ùå NOT a therapist - this is a chatbot, not a licensed mental health professional
  • ‚ùå Cannot handle crises (will direct you to 988 or local resources)
  • ‚ùå May occasionally generate incorrect or nonsensical responses ("hallucinations")
  • ‚ùå Only remembers last 10 messages in a conversation

Safety: 95% crisis detection rate. If you mention self-harm or suicide, the chatbot immediately provides crisis resources.

Our AI Ethics Principles

  1. Transparency: We explain how our AI works in plain English
  2. User Control: You can turn off any AI feature, export your data, or delete your account anytime
  3. No Discrimination: Our models are tested for bias across age, gender, ethnicity, and language
  4. Privacy First: We never use your wellness data for advertising. Your data is encrypted and never sold
  5. Human Oversight: AI is a tool, not a replacement for human judgment. We encourage you to seek professional help when needed
  6. Continuous Improvement: We monitor our models for accuracy, fairness, and safety
  7. Honest About Limitations: We don't overclaim. Our AI is helpful, but it's not perfect, and it's not a substitute for medical care

Frequently Asked Questions

Can your AI diagnose me?

No. Our AI provides informational insights and personalized recommendations. It cannot diagnose medical or psychiatric conditions.

How accurate is your AI?

It varies by model (see accuracy sections above). Generally, 70-85% of users find our AI helpful and accurate. But it's not perfect.

Can I opt out of AI features?

Yes. You can turn off specific features in Settings ‚Üí Features.

Do you use my data to train your AI?

We use aggregated, anonymized data to improve our models. Your individual data is never shared or sold.

What if the AI gives me bad advice?

Please report it immediately (Settings ‚Üí Support ‚Üí Report Issue). We review all reports and update our models accordingly.

Is your AI biased?

We test for bias and work to minimize it, but no AI is perfectly unbiased. If you experience bias, please report it.
